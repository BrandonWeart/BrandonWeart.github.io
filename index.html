Research

This section answers: What problems do you work on? and What methods do you use? Write this like an abstract: concrete + scannable.

Research themes

  • Generative downscaling: score-based diffusion, conditional generation, transfer learning.
  • Severe hazards: hail proxies, convective mode classification, extreme precipitation.
  • Pattern discovery: SOMs, clustering, regime composites, seasonality & teleconnections.
  • Verification: FSS, Brier score, reliability, PSD/PDF matching, tail diagnostics.

Datasets & tools

  • Data: ERA5, GEFS/ECMWF-style ensembles, WRF/CP-RCM outputs, radar/QPE/QPF.
  • Formats: NetCDF, GRIB2 (cfgrib), Zarr, cloud-optimized workflows.
  • Stack: Python, xarray/dask, PyTorch, Cartopy/Matplotlib, Git, HPC schedulers.

Representative questions I ask

  • Can diffusion models reproduce extreme precipitation tails without collapsing variance?
  • How do we design baselines (e.g., smoothing/coarsen/recover) that isolate what ML adds?
  • Do learned regimes correspond to known dynamical drivers (jet shifts, trough/ridge patterns)?
  • What is the minimum conditioning needed to prevent “pretty but wrong” downscaling?
